The Ramayan written by Tulsida known as Ramchartmanas, is a sixteenth century epic. The story revolves around Rama, who was the seventh avatar of the Hindu god Vishnu. He is the central figure of the Hindu epic Ramayana, which is the princial narration of the events connected to His incarnation on Earth, His ideals and His greatness. He was the eldest son of King Dashratha- the ruler of Koasala Kingdom.Rama's Life and journey is one of adherence to dharma despite harsh tests and obstacles and many pains of life and time. This adherence made him earn the of Maryada Purushottam which literally means The Perfect man or lord of Self Control or Lord of Virtune.For the sake of his father's honour,Rama abandoms his claim to Ayodhya's throne to serve an exile of fourteen years in forest. His wife Sita and brother Lakshamana decide to join him, and all three spend the fourteen years in exile together. While in exile, Sita is kidnapped by Ravana, the king of Lanka. After a long and arduos search, Rama fights a colossal war against Ravana's armies. In a war of powerful and magical beings, greatly destructive weaponry and battles, Rama slays Ravana in battle and liberates his wife. Having completed his exile, Rama returns to be crowned king in Ayodhya and eventually becomes the emperor. The whole story is divided into seven parts Known as 'Kands'.
